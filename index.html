Healthy BMI Range

Method Used

WHO BMI Classification

The World Health Organization (WHO) BMI Classification is one of the most widely used methods for assessing body weight relative to height.

What does this mean?

BMI is a screening tool that estimates body size.

Recommended Focus

  • Maintain healthy habits
  • Exercise regularly
  • Eat a balanced diet

Important

BMI is an estimate and does not account for muscle mass, bone density, body composition, age, or ethnicity. It should be used as a general screening tool rather than a medical diagnosis.

Healthy Body Fat Range

—

Method Used

Deurenberg Formula

The Deurenberg Formula estimates body fat percentage using BMI, age, and biological sex.

What does this mean?

Body fat is an estimate and may differ from direct measurement methods.

Recommended Focus

  • Maintain healthy habits
  • Regular exercise
  • Balanced nutrition

Important

Body fat percentage estimated using formulas may differ from direct measurement methods such as DEXA Scan, Hydrostatic Weighing, or Skinfold Measurements. Use this estimate as a general guideline.

Ideal Weight Calculator

Estimated Ideal Weight

—

Enter your height to calculate your estimated ideal weight.

Method Used

Robinson Formula (1983)

This estimate is calculated using the Robinson Formula (1983), a commonly used method for estimating ideal body weight based on height and biological sex.

Important

Ideal weight formulas provide general estimates. Your overall health depends on many factors including body composition, muscle mass, genetics, nutrition, physical activity, and lifestyle. This calculator should be used as a general guide rather than a strict health target.
